# Street Fighter 6 Strategy Builder Design for EsportsAiScout

The **Street Fighter 6 Strategy Builder** is a comprehensive tool within the EsportsAiScout app aimed at helping players and coaches develop data-driven gameplans. By leveraging match data and AI analysis, it generates **scouting reports, counter-pick strategies, punishes, and visual heatmaps** to exploit an opponent‚Äôs tendencies. This feature is optimized for use **before matches (prep phase)** and **during matches** (quick insights between rounds) to elevate decision-making in real time. As fighting games like SF6 have a *‚Äústrong data-to-strategy loop‚Äù*, the Strategy Builder harnesses that loop to give players a competitive edge in a joyful, clear UI.

## 1. Auto-Generated Opponent Scouting Reports

**Purpose:** Provide a **natural-language summary** of the opponent‚Äôs playstyle tendencies, derived from their past match data. This mimics the way top players ‚Äúdownload‚Äù an opponent‚Äôs habits, but faster.

- **Data Extraction:** The system analyzes **replay and statistical data** from the opponent‚Äôs recent matches to quantify their behavior:
  - **Aggression vs. Defense:** Are they rushdown aggressive or defensive and reactive? For example, the tool can track how often they **move forward vs. backward**, time spent cornering the opponent, or how frequently they initiate attacks versus block.
  - **Wakeup Habits:** What do they do on knockdown? If data shows they ‚Äúwake up‚Äù (rise from knockdown) with an invincible **Shoryuken (DP) reversal 40% of the time**, that‚Äôs a risky habit to exploit. The report might note *‚Äútends to perform an invincible reversal on wakeup frequently ‚Äì bait this out for a big punish.‚Äù*
  - **Throw Tech Frequency:** Track how often they successfully **tech throws** vs. get thrown. A very high throw-tech rate could indicate they are twitchy on defense ‚Äì the report could say *‚Äúdefensively, they tech throws ~80% of the time, making them vulnerable to shimmy (walk-back bait) techniques.‚Äù*
  - **Jump-In Rate and Anti-Air**: Measure how often they jump at the opponent and how often they get **anti-aired**. For instance, *‚Äúoften starts rounds with jump-in attacks‚Äù*. If they jump frequently but have poor success (e.g. getting anti-aired a lot) it shows reckless approach; if they **jump and don‚Äôt get anti-aired** (perhaps the opponent failed to counter), it suggests their opponents struggled to counter them. A high success rate on jump-ins might indicate their past foes didn‚Äôt punish aerial approaches ‚Äì something to be ready for. (E.g. *‚ÄúLoves jump-in approaches, but over 50% of his jumps go unpunished ‚Äì be prepared with anti-airs.‚Äù*)
  - **Combo Execution and Drops:** Note their **execution consistency** ‚Äì do they land big combos or frequently drop them under pressure? For example: *‚Äútends to drop longer combos in clutch moments, indicating nerves.‚Äù* This insight can affect how much you respect their damage output.
  - **Resource Usage:** Analyze how they use SF6‚Äôs **Drive Gauge and Super meter**. Do they frequently enter **Burnout** by overusing the Drive gauge? Do they save up Super meter without using it (missed opportunities)? For example: *‚ÄúOften burns out by round end ‚Äì consider pressuring when they‚Äôre low on Drive gauge.‚Äù* Efficient or poor meter use is a key tendency.
  - **Adaptive Tendencies:** The report highlights if the opponent adjusts between rounds or is very set in their ways. Some players adapt (download complete) while others stick to one gameplan. For instance: *‚ÄúDoes not vary tactics much between rounds ‚Äì likely to repeat patterns even after being punished.‚Äù*

- **Natural Language Summary:** The tool outputs a concise scouting report in sentences, translating data into **human-readable insights**. For example:
  - *‚Äú**Playstyle:** Very aggressive opener ‚Äì often starts with a jump-in or dash punch. Tends to rely on high-risk moves (frequent wake-up DP) when pressured. Defensively, this player techs throws consistently but struggles to anti-air jumps.‚Äù*
  - *‚Äú**Habits:** On knockdown, they **often reversal DP** (approx. 50% of knockdowns). In neutral, they favor mid-range pokes but will **jump if fireballed**. They rarely use their character‚Äôs projectile, preferring close combat (e.g. a Ken player who hardly throws fireballs).‚Äù*
  - *‚Äú**Exploitable Tendencies:** Blocks low a lot but is vulnerable to overheads; tends to jump back from corner (corner escape attempt can be predicted).‚Äù*

- **Highlight Exploits:** The system bolds or color-codes parts of the report that indicate a weakness **you can exploit**. For example, if the opponent *‚Äúoften presses buttons after blocking two hits‚Äù*, the tool might highlight this as *‚Äú**Habits:** often mashes after two hits ‚Äì exploit with frame traps.‚Äù* By automatically identifying such patterns, even subtle ones, the AI scout surfaces opportunities a player can capitalize on.

These scouting insights mirror what a professional coach might tell a player before a match, condensing many matches worth of data into a quick briefing.

## 2. Counter-Pick Suggestions and Punishes

**Purpose:** Recommend optimal **character choices** and specific **tactical punishes** to counter the opponent‚Äôs main character and habits. This feature is driven by matchup statistics and move data to maximize the player‚Äôs advantage.

- **Counter-Pick Character Advice:** The Strategy Builder looks at the opponent‚Äôs chosen character and suggests which of *your* available characters would statistically have an edge. This is based on **matchup win-rate data** from high-level play (e.g. community-sourced matchup charts and official stats). For example, if the opponent plays **Zangief** (a slow grappler), the tool might suggest picking a **zoning character** like JP or Guile, who have favorable matchups against Zangief (due to zoning tools that keep grapplers out). It would say something like: *‚Äú**Counter-Pick:** Consider using Guile ‚Äì historically strong vs. Zangief with a ~6-4 matchup advantage (projectile zoning nullifies Gief‚Äôs approach).‚Äù* The recommendation is data-driven: e.g. referencing **CatCammy‚Äôs SF6 matchup table** which compiles high-level ranked data or Capcom‚Äôs official **‚ÄúBattle Diagram‚Äù** win rates. This gives confidence that the suggestion isn‚Äôt just theorycraft, but backed by aggregated results.

  - If the player only uses one main character (no secondary picks), this advice can still help by highlighting what **that character** needs to be mindful of. E.g. *‚ÄúYour character has a slight disadvantage in this matchup ‚Äì expect to work harder. Focus on spacing and whiff punishing, as that‚Äôs where this matchup can swing back.‚Äù* In teams where character switch is possible (or casual play), the counter-pick info becomes directly actionable.

- **Key Punishes for Opponent‚Äôs Moves:** Using frame data knowledge and the opponent‚Äôs habits, the tool lists a few **specific punishes or counters**:
  - It identifies the opponent‚Äôs **signature moves or frequent attacks** ‚Äì for instance, if the opponent is a Ken player who **spams heavy Dragon Punch (DP)** or a Juri player doing a lot of unsafe **sweeps**, the system will list optimal punish combos. e.g. *‚ÄúPunish **blocked DP** with: Jump-in HK, cr.MP, DP for max damage‚Äù*, or *‚ÄúOn blocking Juri‚Äôs sweep, immediately punish with cr.HP xx Hadoken‚Äù*. These punishes are based on frame data ‚Äì a blocked sweep is usually very unsafe, so a heavy attack punish is guaranteed.
  - It can also suggest **universal counters** for certain strategies: *‚ÄúIf opponent uses Drive Impact repeatedly, use Drive Parry or your own Drive Impact to counter.‚Äù* Or *‚ÄúBait **throws** by shimmying (step back to let their throw whiff, then punish).‚Äù* Each suggestion ties to a specific habit identified in the scouting report. For example, *‚Äútends to throw a lot -> punish by neutral jumping: jump straight up on their wakeup to avoid throw and get a full jump-in combo.‚Äù*
  - The tool leverages a knowledge base of common **move interactions** (akin to a coach‚Äôs matchup notes). It might say: *‚ÄúKimberly‚Äôs run -> slide is unsafe on block; be ready to block low and get a free combo.‚Äù* These tips ensure the player is prepared to capitalize on the opponent‚Äôs go-to moves.

- **Opening Round Strategy:** The Strategy Builder even provides a tip for the **round start** situation, since many players have a favored round opener:
  - It looks at what the opponent does at the very start of rounds (if data is available). For example, *‚ÄúThis opponent favors backdashing at round start 70% of the time.‚Äù* Knowing this, it might suggest *‚Äúopen with a fast **Drive Rush** forward to close the gap immediately‚Äù* or *‚Äúuse a long-range poke at round start to catch the backdash.‚Äù* If the opponent often opens with a jump or a fireball, the advice could be *‚Äústart by walking and blocking, ready to anti-air if they jump‚Äù*.
  - These immediate tactics set the tone and can score an early hit. Even a small edge like a predicted backdash can yield a whiff punish. By including this in the strategy, the player goes into the match with a gameplan for **‚ÄúFight!‚Äù** as soon as the announcer calls it.

- **Adaptive Suggestions:** Because matches evolve, the counter strategy can update if used *during* a set:
  - For example, after one game, if the tool notices the opponent is abusing a certain tactic (maybe they weren‚Äôt using that in previous data but pulled it out now), it can suggest a mid-set adjustment. *‚ÄúOpponent has started using Drive Impact frequently this set ‚Äì look to neutral jump or Drive Reversal to counter it.‚Äù* 
  - This dynamic aspect ensures the Strategy Builder isn‚Äôt a static pre-match scout only, but an active coach that **updates its advice as new data comes in** (if the app is used live and can record match events between rounds).

By combining **who** you play (character choice) with **how** you play (specific punishes and tactics), the Strategy Builder covers strategic decisions from the macro level down to the micro level.

## 3. Visual Heatmaps of Move Zones

**Purpose:** Present visual **diagrams (heatmaps)** that illustrate where on the screen or at what ranges the opponent tends to perform certain actions. This gives a spatial sense of their habits ‚Äì effectively showing their ‚Äúhot zones‚Äù and vulnerabilities.

- **Heatmap Generation:** The system processes match replays (or live tracking data) to record positions and actions:
  - Street Fighter 6 is a 2D game, so one way to map activity is by the **horizontal distance or screen position**. We can imagine the stage divided into segments (e.g., Left Corner, Mid-range, Right Corner, or simply distance between fighters measured in in-game units).
  - For each segment or distance range, the tool logs frequency of key actions by the opponent: **projectiles thrown, normals whiffed, jumps, dashes, etc.** For example, it might find that *‚Äúat roughly 3 character-lengths apart (mid-screen), the opponent throws a high number of fireballs,‚Äù* whereas *‚Äúat close range, they attempt many throws.‚Äù*
  - These frequencies are then plotted as a heatmap grid. One axis could represent the **horizontal position or distance** and the other axis the **type of action**. The cell colors (from cool blue to hot red) indicate how often that action occurs at that position.

- **Diagram Example:** Imagine a heatmap where the X-axis is **Position on Stage** (left to right, or distance from opponent) and Y-axis is **Action**:
  - One row might be ‚ÄúFireball thrown‚Äù. It could show red in the middle columns (meaning the opponent commonly throws fireballs from mid-screen) and blue in the corner columns (meaning they rarely throw fireballs when cornered or at point-blank range).
  - Another row ‚ÄúHeavy Punch whiff‚Äù might show a hotspot at a specific range, indicating the opponent often whiffs heavy punches when you‚Äôre just outside their reach (classic footsies trap).
  - ‚ÄúJump Attempts‚Äù might light up at mid-range if the opponent likes to jump over fireballs from that distance.
  - **Safe vs Danger Zones:** Using this, the tool can explicitly mark **‚Äúdanger zones‚Äù** (where the opponent is most effective) and **‚Äúsafe zones‚Äù** (where the opponent is less comfortable or where their moves don‚Äôt reach). For instance, *‚ÄúThe heatmap shows your opponent is most dangerous at close range (lots of throw and DP attempts in red). Conversely, at long range, they have no presence (blue) ‚Äì a safe zone to reposition or throw projectiles.‚Äù*

- **Interpretation:** Alongside the graphic, the Strategy Builder includes a short interpretation:
  - *‚ÄúVisual Analysis: Notice the **red cluster in mid-screen for jump-ins** ‚Äì this player frequently jump attacks from that range. Be ready with anti-airs when you‚Äôre at mid distance. In contrast, their **fireball game is hot at full screen** but cools off at close range, meaning they use projectiles to control distance but abandon them up close.‚Äù*
  - *‚ÄúSafe Zone: Outside sweep range (~ beyond ¬Ω screen) ‚Äì the opponent has few threats here (they rarely approach without a projectile). Danger Zone: Corner ‚Äì when cornered, they wildly attempt escape (see red in ‚Äòjump‚Äô and ‚ÄòDP‚Äô in corner positions). Keep them cornered but watch for desperate moves.‚Äù*
  - Such insights help players spatially understand how to position themselves. Against someone with a strong mid-range, you either want to be closer (inside their optimal range) or further (outside their range). The heatmap makes these tendencies **immediately visible**.

- **Mock Heatmap Data (for integration):** To integrate into our app, the heatmap data might be structured as JSON. For example, we could use a grid representation:
  ```json
  {
    "xLabels": ["Left Corner", "Near Range", "Mid Range", "Far Range", "Right Corner"],
    "yLabels": ["Fireball", "Whiffed Normal", "Jump"],
    "data": [
      [1, 0, 5, 2, 0],   // Fireball frequencies at each zone
      [0, 3, 5, 1, 0],   // Whiffed Normal frequencies
      [0, 1, 4, 1, 0]    // Jump frequencies
    ]
  }
  ```
  In this example, the opponent threw 5 fireballs at mid range (red zone), and none in the corners (blue zones). They whiffed normals mostly at near-to-mid ranges. They attempted several jumps at mid range too. This JSON can feed a React heatmap component (like **react-heatmap-grid** or similar) to render the colored grid.

The heatmaps add a **visual, intuitive layer** to the scouting report. Even at a glance, a player can see ‚Äúwhere on the screen should I be careful?‚Äù and ‚Äúwhere can I fight them on my terms?‚Äù This is a novel approach inspired by other sports ‚Äì for instance, Fightmetric‚Äôs MMA heatmaps show where fights take place, and we apply the concept to a 2D fighting game arena.

## 5. Real-Time Use During Matches

The Strategy Builder is not just a pre-game tool; it‚Äôs designed to assist **during a match** without causing distraction or delays. Here‚Äôs how it can function in various scenarios:

- **Between Rounds:** Street Fighter matches typically have short breaks between rounds (a few seconds) and a longer break between games in a set. The app can display a **‚Äúbetween-round summary‚Äù** on a secondary screen (or to a coach):
  - This summary might include one **key adjustment tip** (in large text for quick reading). For example, *‚ÄúTip: He‚Äôs jumped at you 3 times in Round 1 ‚Äì anti-air those!‚Äù* or *‚ÄúTip: You‚Äôve thrown 2 fireballs and he Drive Impacted through both ‚Äì use feints or be ready to cancel to parry.‚Äù*

  - The UI can be as simple as a pop-up or overlay. Perhaps a small overlay on the game feed (if using PC, like a transparent window showing the tip) or a notification on a smartwatch/phone for the coach. We ensure it‚Äôs **non-intrusive and quick to read** (big font, minimal text).

- **Between Games:** In tournament sets (best of 3 or 5 games), between games there‚Äôs often a longer break (players may take 30-60 seconds, sometimes even consult a coach). Here, the Strategy Builder can be consulted for a slightly more detailed recap:

- **Post-Match Report:** Although not exactly ‚Äúduring‚Äù the match, it‚Äôs worth noting the tool can generate a **comprehensive report after the match** combining all these insights. By functioning as a smart ‚Äúassistant coach‚Äù in real-time, the Strategy Builder can **improve adaptation and decision-making on the fly**, which is exactly what defines top players in Street Fighter.
